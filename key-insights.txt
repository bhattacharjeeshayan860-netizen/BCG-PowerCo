**Customer Churn Analysis & Predictive Modeling – Executive Summary**

**Objective**
Analyze the drivers of customer churn and build a predictive model to identify customers at risk of leaving, with a particular focus on the impact of electricity price changes.

**Key Findings**

* A Random Forest churn prediction model was developed using customer, contract, consumption, and pricing features.
* The model can successfully identify customers likely to churn, enabling proactive retention strategies.
* Price volatility (changes in electricity prices) shows a measurable relationship with churn, indicating that customers are sensitive to fluctuations in energy pricing.
* Additional drivers of churn include contract renewal timing, customer tenure, and consumption behavior.

**Business Impact**

* The model enables the company to identify **high-risk customers before they churn**, allowing targeted retention campaigns.
* Focusing retention efforts on high-probability churn customers can significantly reduce revenue loss and improve customer lifetime value.
* By proactively offering incentives (e.g., contract adjustments or pricing offers), the company can retain valuable SME customers and stabilize revenue streams.

**Recommended Next Steps**

1. Deploy the churn prediction model to score customers on a regular basis.
2. Prioritize high-risk customers for targeted retention initiatives (e.g., contract renegotiation, pricing incentives).
3. Monitor price changes closely and evaluate pricing strategies to reduce churn risk.
4. Continuously retrain and improve the model as new customer and pricing data becomes available.

**Conclusion**
The analysis demonstrates that price dynamics and contract factors influence churn behavior. Implementing a predictive churn model will allow the company to move from reactive to proactive customer retention, improving long-term revenue stability.
